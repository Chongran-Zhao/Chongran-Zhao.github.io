Research Details

1. Finite-Strain Viscoelasticity Theory

We define generalized strains as $\bm E:=\sum_{a=1}^3 E(\lambda_a)\bm M_a$, where $E(\lambda)$ is a scale function. The kinematic decomposition follows $\bm E:=\bm E^{\mathrm{e}}+\bm E^{\mathrm{v}}$ with $\bm E^{\mathrm{v}}\in\mathrm{Sym}(3)_+$ serving as an independent internal variable that represents viscous deformation.

2. Model Calibration

We calibrate hyperelastic and viscoelastic models to experiments and include damage effects (Mullins effect) when necessary.
