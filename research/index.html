1. Finite-Strain Viscoelasticity Theory

We define generalized strains as $\boldsymbol E:=\sum_{a=1}^3 E(\lambda_a)\boldsymbol M_a$, where $E(\lambda)$ is a scale function and $\boldsymbol M_a$ are spectral projectors. The Green-Naghdi-type kinematics leads to the additive split \(\boldsymbol E:=\boldsymbol E^{\mathrm{e}}+\boldsymbol E^{\mathrm{v}}\), with \(\boldsymbol E^{\mathrm{v}}\in\mathrm{Sym}(3)_+\) treated as an independent internal variable representing viscous deformation.

This framework supports both linear and nonlinear regimes. In the linear limit it recovers hereditary-integral behavior, and in the nonlinear regime it enables calibratable evolution of inelastic deformation.
